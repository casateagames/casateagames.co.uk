How it started

It began with home-brewed rules (and tapas)

We didn’t start out planning to make games. We started out home-brewing them - tweaking rules, remixing mechanics, and saying “what if we tried it like this?” whenever friends came over.

People kept asking the same thing: “Have you thought about making your own board game?”

Then one lunch, over bottomless tapas at Las Iguanas, it clicked. Casa Tea Games was born - casa meaning home, tea meaning brew - a place to test ideas until they’re fun. Also, this is way too much tapas for two people!

That same day, fueled by many a small dish, we were creating prototype decks for Say What Now?!, researching drawing tablets, and accidentally turning our dining table into carnage.

A quick confession

Board game playing and board game collecting are two different hobbies. In a boring world we’d open the shrink-wrapped ones… but instead we’re adding to the shelf with our own.
